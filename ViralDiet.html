Viral Fever Management Guide

Diet

A balanced diet is essential during viral fever to support recovery. Here are some dietary recommendations:

  • Hydration:
    • Drink plenty of water, herbal teas, coconut water, and clear broths.
    • Consider oral rehydration solutions (ORS) if dehydrated.
  • Light and Nutritious Foods:
    • Fruits: Focus on vitamin C-rich fruits like oranges and bananas.
    • Vegetables: Eat steamed or boiled vegetables (e.g., carrots, spinach).
    • Soups: Include clear soups or broths for hydration and nutrients.
  • Proteins: Consume lean proteins like boiled eggs, chicken, or fish; plant-based options like lentils are also good. Include yogurt for probiotics.
  • Complex Carbohydrates: Opt for plain rice, oats, or whole-grain toast for energy.
  • Avoid Heavy Foods: Limit fried, spicy, or greasy foods, and avoid sugary snacks.
  • Small, Frequent Meals: Eat smaller portions throughout the day to aid digestion.
  • Herbal Remedies: Consider ginger tea for nausea and honey with warm lemon for soothing.

This diet supports recovery by focusing on hydration and essential nutrients. Always consult a healthcare provider for personalized advice.
