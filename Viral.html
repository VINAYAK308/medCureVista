Viral Fever Management Guide

Medication

Common medications for managing viral fever primarily focus on relieving symptoms, as antibiotics are ineffective against viruses. Here are some commonly used medications:

  • Acetaminophen (Tylenol): Reduces fever and alleviates pain.
  • Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin): Anti-inflammatory that can help lower fever and relieve pain.
  • Aspirin: Used for pain and fever reduction, but generally not recommended for children or teenagers due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.
  • Decongestants: Help relieve nasal congestion associated with viral infections (e.g., pseudoephedrine).
  • Antihistamines: Can help alleviate symptoms like runny nose and sneezing (e.g., diphenhydramine).

It's important to consult a healthcare professional before taking any medication, especially for children or if there are underlying health conditions.
