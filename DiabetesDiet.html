Diabetes Management Guide

Diet

  • Balanced Meals: Focus on balanced meals that include carbohydrates, proteins, and healthy fats to help stabilize blood sugar levels.
  • Carbohydrate Management: Choose complex carbohydrates with a low glycemic index (GI) such as whole grains, legumes, and non-starchy vegetables.
  • Fruits and Vegetables: Aim for a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables. Choose fresh or frozen options without added sugars.
  • Lean Proteins: Include lean protein sources such as chicken, turkey, fish, tofu, legumes, and low-fat dairy.
  • Healthy Fats: Incorporate healthy fats from sources like avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil.
  • Limit Added Sugars: Avoid sugary drinks, sweets, and processed foods high in added sugars.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day; limit sugary beverages and alcohol.
  • Regular Meal Patterns: Eat regular meals and snacks to help maintain stable blood sugar levels.
  • Monitor Blood Sugar: Regularly check blood sugar levels to understand how different foods affect your body.
  • Consult a Dietitian: Work with a registered dietitian or healthcare provider for personalized meal planning and advice.
