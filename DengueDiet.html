Dengue Management Guide

Diet

  • Hydration
    • Fluids: Drink plenty of water, coconut water, and clear broths to prevent dehydration.
    • Oral Rehydration Solutions (ORS): Consider ORS if dehydration is a concern.
  • Nutritious Foods
    • Fruits: Include vitamin C-rich fruits like oranges, guavas, and papayas to boost immunity and help with recovery.
    • Vegetables: Eat well-cooked vegetables such as carrots, spinach, and pumpkin, which are easy to digest.
  • Proteins
    • Lean Proteins: Consume easily digestible proteins like boiled eggs, chicken, or fish. Plant-based options like lentils and beans are also beneficial.
    • Dairy: Include yogurt for probiotics, which can help with gut health.
  • Complex Carbohydrates
    • Whole Grains: Choose plain rice, oats, or whole-grain bread to provide energy.
  • Avoid Heavy Foods
    • Limit Fried and Spicy Foods: Avoid oily, greasy, or spicy dishes that may irritate the stomach.
    • Avoid Sugary Foods: Steer clear of sugary snacks and beverages.
  • Small, Frequent Meals
    • Eat Small Portions: Have smaller, more frequent meals to aid digestion and maintain energy levels.
  • Herbal Remedies
    • Ginger Tea: Ginger can help soothe nausea and improve digestion.
    • Honey and Lemon: A mixture of honey and warm lemon water can be soothing and provide energy.
