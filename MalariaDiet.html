Malaria Management Guide

Diet

1. Hydration

  • Fluids: Drink plenty of water, coconut water, and herbal teas to stay hydrated.
  • Oral Rehydration Solutions (ORS): Use ORS if dehydration is a concern.

2. Nutritious Foods

  • Fruits: Include vitamin C-rich fruits like oranges, lemons, and guavas to boost immunity.
  • Vegetables: Eat a variety of well-cooked vegetables, such as spinach, carrots, and broccoli.

3. Proteins

  • Lean Proteins: Consume easily digestible proteins like boiled eggs, chicken, and fish. Lentils and beans are good plant-based options.
  • Dairy: Include yogurt for probiotics and digestive health.

4. Complex Carbohydrates

  • Whole Grains: Choose plain rice, oats, or whole-grain bread for energy.

5. Avoid Heavy Foods

  • Limit Fried and Spicy Foods: Avoid greasy, oily, or spicy dishes that may irritate the stomach.
  • Avoid Sugary Foods: Steer clear of sugary snacks and beverages.

6. Small, Frequent Meals

  • Eat Small Portions: Have smaller, more frequent meals to maintain energy levels and aid digestion.

7. Herbal Remedies

  • Ginger Tea: Ginger can help soothe nausea and improve digestion.
  • Honey and Lemon: A mixture of honey and warm lemon water can provide energy and support hydration.

Conclusion: This diet focuses on hydration, essential nutrients, and easily digestible foods to support recovery from malaria. Always consult a healthcare provider for personalized dietary recommendations.
