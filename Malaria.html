Malaria Management Guide

1. Medication

The medication for malaria depends on the type of malaria, the severity of the disease, and the region where the infection was acquired. Common antimalarial medications include:

  • Chloroquine: Used to treat uncomplicated malaria caused by Plasmodium vivax, P. ovale, and P. malariae.
  • Artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs): First-line treatments for P. falciparum malaria. Common combinations include:
    • Artemether-lumefantrine (Coartem)
    • Artesunate-amodiaquine
    • Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine
  • Quinine: Used for severe malaria, often in combination with other medications.
  • Mefloquine: Used for both treatment and prevention of malaria, particularly in areas where P. falciparum is resistant to chloroquine.
  • Atovaquone-proguanil (Malarone): Used for both treatment and prevention, effective against P. falciparum.
  • Primaquine: Used to treat and prevent relapse in cases of P. vivax and P. ovale.

Note: It’s essential to consult a healthcare professional for the appropriate medication based on the specific circumstances of the infection.
